# Gainesville - Place To Go

One of the top college towns in the country, [Gainesville](https://www.visitgainesville.com/), located in [North Central Florida](https://www.visitflorida.com/places-to-go/north-central/) an hour southwest of [Jacksonville](https://www.visitflorida.com/places-to-go/northeast/jacksonville/), is home to the [University of Florida](https://www.visitflorida.com/travel-ideas/articles/florida-tours-campus-attractions-university-of-florida/) and the [Florida Gators](https://floridagators.com/sports/football). In addition to the excitement of [college sports](https://floridagators.com/), Gainesville offers outstanding [art galleries](https://www.visitgainesville.com/explore/category/art-galleries/) and [cultural centers](https://www.visitgainesville.com/things-to-do/museums-galleries-and-art-walks/), abundant [outdoor recreation](https://www.visitgainesville.com/explore/get-outdoors/) opportunities, plenty of [places to shop](https://www.visitgainesville.com/things-to-do/shopping/), and a diverse [culinary scene](https://www.visitgainesville.com/dine/) sure to please everyone from foodies to young families.

Can't-miss attractions include the [Florida Museum of Natural History](https://www.visitflorida.com/listing/florida-museum-of-natural-history/30205/), [Harn Museum of Art](https://www.visitflorida.com/listing/harn-museum-of-art/26991/), and [Kanapaha Botanical Gardens](https://kanapaha.org/). Visitors can find special [events](https://www.visitgainesville.com/events/) going on throughout the year with annual festivals including [Downtown Festival & Art Show](https://www.gainesvilledowntownartfest.net/), [Hoggetowne Medieval Faire](https://www.hoggetownemedfaire.com/), [Spring Arts Festival](https://www.sfcollege.edu/spring-arts/), and [The Fest](https://thefestfl.com/). Outside of [college sports](https://floridagators.com/), the Gainesville area is home to a wide variety of other [sporting events and venues](https://www.visitgainesville.com/sports/), including the [Alachua County Agriculture and Equestrian Center](https://www.visitgainesville.com/explore/dressage-drill-teams-morgans-and-more-at-the-alachua-county-agriculture-and-equestrian-center/) and the [Gainesville Raceway](https://gainesvilleraceway.com/), "Florida's home for drag racing."

From [hiking](https://www.visitgainesville.com/outdoor-recreation/hiking/) and [biking](https://www.visitgainesville.com/outdoor-recreation/biking-trails-one/) to [paddling](https://www.visitgainesville.com/outdoor-recreation/paddling-trails/) and watersports, outdoor adventures can take many forms in Gainesville's [parks](https://www.visitgainesville.com/outdoor-recreation/parks-places/) and preserves, including [Newnans Lake State Forest](https://www.fdacs.gov/Forest-Wildfire/Our-Forests/State-Forests/Newnans-Lake-State-Forest2), [Sweetwater Wetlands Park](https://www.gainesvillefl.gov/Parks/Sweetwater-Wetlands-Park), and [Palm Point Nature Park](https://www.gainesvillefl.gov/Parks/Palm-Point-Nature-Park). [O'Leno State Park](https://www.floridastateparks.org/parks-and-trails/oleno-state-park) and [River Rise State Park](https://www.floridastateparks.org/parks-and-trails/river-rise-preserve-state-park/experiences-amenities) in [High Springs](https://www.visitflorida.com/places-to-go/north-central/high-springs/) are just a 30-minute drive away, and [Paynes Prairie Preserve State Park](https://www.floridastateparks.org/parks-and-trails/paynes-prairie-preserve-state-park) is a quick 10-minute drive south of Gainesville in [Micanopy](https://www.visitflorida.com/places-to-go/north-central/micanopy/). Gainesville also offers convenient access to the [popular springs](https://www.visitgainesville.com/explore/splash-into-the-springs-near-gainesville-fl/) of nearby communities, including [Gilchrist Blue Springs](https://www.floridastateparks.org/parks-and-trails/ruth-b-kirby-gilchrist-blue-springs-state-park), [Poe Springs](https://alachuacounty.us/Depts/parks/Pages/Details.aspx?park=Poe%20Springs%20Park), [Ginnie Springs](https://ginniespringsoutdoors.com/), [Rum Island Spring](https://www.naturalnorthflorida.com/things-to-do/rum-island-spring-and-park/) and [Ichetucknee Springs](https://www.floridastateparks.org/parks-and-trails/ichetucknee-springs-state-park) for tubing, diving, snorkeling, and swimming.

When it comes to [dining](https://www.visitgainesville.com/dine/) and [shopping](https://www.visitgainesville.com/things-to-do/shopping/), visitors can find just about anything they're looking for at the more than 150 stores and 2 million square feet of retail and restaurant space of the [Neighborhoods of Butler](https://www.visitgainesville.com/explore/attraction/butler-enterprises/). [Celebration Pointe](https://www.celebrationpointe.com/) and [Tioga Town Center](https://www.tiogatowncenter.com/) offer a blend of retail establishments, eateries, and entertainment within walkable areas, and national chain retailers can be found among the more than 100 stores of [The Oaks Mall](https://www.theoaksmall.com/en.html).

Plan your trip at [Visit Gainesville](https://www.visitgainesville.com/).

## FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS ABOUT GAINESVILLE

Q. Where is Gainesville?

A. Gainesville is located in the north-central part of the state. It serves as the county seat of Alachua County and is known for being home to the University of Florida. The city is approximately 70 miles southwest of Jacksonville and 115 miles northwest of Orlando. Gainesville is easily accessible via Interstate 75, which runs north-south through the city.  Here's where you can see [Gainesville on the map](https://www.google.com/maps/place/Gainesville,+FL/@29.5296241,-82.8958011,155208m/data=!3m1!1e3!4m6!3m5!1s0x88e6202169bf2c2b:0x80e7795d28108d96!8m2!3d29.6519563!4d-82.324998!16zL20vMHJoN3Q?entry=ttu&g_ep=EgoyMDI1MTAwOC4wIKXMDSoASAFQAw%3D%3D).

Q. What county is Gainesville in?

A. Gainesville is located in Alachua County. It is the largest city in the county and serves as the county seat. Alachua County is known for its natural beauty, including numerous parks and nature preserves. The county is also home to several cultural and educational institutions, including the University of Florida.

Q. How far is Gainesville from Orlando?

A. Gainesville is approximately 115 miles northwest of Orlando. The drive typically takes about two hours via Interstate 75 and Florida's Turnpike. This makes Gainesville a convenient day trip or weekend getaway for those visiting Orlando. The route offers scenic views of Florida's countryside and small towns along the way.

Q. What is there to do in Gainesville?

A. Gainesville offers a variety of activities for visitors. For outdoor enthusiasts, [Kanapaha Botanical Gardens](https://kanapaha.org/) and [Paynes Prairie Preserve State Park](https://www.floridastateparks.org/parks-and-trails/paynes-prairie-preserve-state-park) provide beautiful landscapes and wildlife viewing opportunities. The city also has a vibrant arts scene, with galleries, theaters, and live music venues.

Q. What are the best outdoor activities in Gainesville?

A. Gainesville is a haven for outdoor enthusiasts. You can enjoy hiking and bird watching at Paynes Prairie Preserve State Park, which offers a unique landscape of wetlands and grasslands. The Gainesville-Hawthorne State Trail is perfect for biking and walking, stretching over 16 miles through scenic areas. For a more leisurely experience, visit Kanapaha Botanical Gardens, where you can stroll through themed gardens. Don't miss Devil's Millhopper Geological State Park, a fascinating sinkhole with a lush, miniature rainforest.

Q. Where can I find the best local food in Gainesville?

A. Gainesville boasts a diverse culinary scene with plenty of local favorites. For a taste of Southern comfort food, visit The Yearling Restaurant, located about 25 minutes away in Hawthorne, known for its authentic dishes. Satchel's Pizza is a must-visit for its unique atmosphere and delicious pies. For farm-to-table dining, The Top offers a menu filled with locally sourced ingredients.

Q. What family-friendly activities are available in Gainesville?

A. Gainesville offers a variety of family-friendly activities. The Cade Museum for Creativity and Invention is a great place for kids to learn through interactive exhibits. The Santa Fe College Teaching Zoo provides a chance to see a variety of animals and learn about conservation efforts. Additionally, Depot Park offers playgrounds, splash pads, and walking trails for a fun day outdoors.

Q. Are there any annual events or festivals in Gainesville?

A. Gainesville hosts several annual events and festivals that draw visitors from all over. The Downtown Festival & Art Show is a popular event featuring artists from across the country. The Hoggetowne Medieval Faire offers a step back in time with jousting, crafts, and entertainment. For music lovers, the New Horizons Fest showcases local and national jazz talent. Additionally, the University of Florida Homecoming Parade is a beloved tradition that brings the community together.

Q. What are the best parks in Gainesville for a picnic?

A. Gainesville has numerous parks perfect for a picnic. Alfred A. Ring Park offers a peaceful setting with walking trails and picnic areas. Loblolly Woods Nature Park is another great spot, known for its natural beauty and shaded areas. For a more urban setting, Depot Park provides picnic tables and a playground, making it ideal for families. Sweetwater Wetlands Park is also a lovely choice, with scenic views and wildlife watching opportunities.

Q. Where can I go hiking near Gainesville?

A. Gainesville is surrounded by excellent hiking opportunities. San Felasco Hammock Preserve State Park offers miles of trails through diverse ecosystems, perfect for both beginners and experienced hikers. Paynes Prairie Preserve State Park features trails that provide stunning views of the prairie and its wildlife. The Gainesville-Hawthorne State Trail is another option, offering a longer trek through beautiful landscapes. For a unique experience, explore the trails at Devil's Millhopper Geological State Park.

Q. What is the best time to visit Gainesville?

A. The best time to visit Gainesville is during the fall and spring months. From September to November, the weather is pleasant. Spring, from March to May, offers mild temperatures and blooming flowers, making it ideal for outdoor activities. These seasons also coincide with various festivals and events, providing plenty of entertainment options. Summers can be hot and humid, while winters are generally mild.

Q. Where can I find camping and campgrounds near Gainesville?

A. There are several camping options near Gainesville for those looking to enjoy the great outdoors. Paynes Prairie Preserve State Park offers campsites with amenities and beautiful natural surroundings.

Q. Are there any RV parks in Gainesville?

A. Yes, Gainesville has several nearby RV parks that cater to travelers. Located half an hour away in Alachua, Travelers Campground is a popular choice, offering full hookups and a range of amenities. Williston Crossings RV Resort, located half an hour away in Williston, provides a luxurious experience with spacious sites and a variety of recreational activities. Additionally, Sun Retreats Ocala Orange Lake, sited 30 minutes away in Citra,offers a unique combination of RV camping and golfing. Each park provides a comfortable base for exploring Gainesville and the surrounding area.

Q. How far is Gainesville from the nearest beach?

A. Gainesville is approximately 75 miles from the nearest beach, making it a convenient day trip destination. One of the closest beaches is Crescent Beach, located on the Atlantic coast near St. Augustine. It typically takes about an hour and a half to drive there via Interstate 95. Crescent Beach offers a quieter, more relaxed atmosphere compared to some of the busier Florida beaches, making it ideal for a peaceful day by the ocean.
