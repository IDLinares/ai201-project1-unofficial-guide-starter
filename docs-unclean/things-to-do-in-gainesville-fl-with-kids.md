## Discover 5 Things to Do in Gainesville, FL, with Kids 

#### **Explore the Outdoors**

We all know little ones have a lot of energy, and that's why a nice stroll in the park can be very beneficial (and fun!) for them. One of our favorite spots in Gainesville is the [Sweetwater Wetlands Park](https://sweetwaterinn.com/blog/sweetwater-wetlands-park/). You can enjoy a scenic walk through the marsh and even spot some wildlife! A little less wild park to visit is [Depot Park](https://sweetwaterinn.com/blog/depot-park-in-gainesville/). It's one of the newest additions to the city and provides a great playground for kids. If it's a hot day, they can splash around at the blue grotto waterfall! 

#### **Visit a Museum**

Did you know Gainesville has a variety of [museums](https://sweetwaterinn.com/blog/gainesville-fl-museums/) you can visit with your children? The [CADE Museum](https://sweetwaterinn.com/blog/cade-museum/), for example, is right by Depot Park, so don't hesitate to start with that one while you're in the area. Another good museum to discover is the [Florida Museum of Natural History](https://sweetwaterinn.com/blog/florida-museum-of-natural-history/). It's one of the top five natural history museums in the country!

#### **Go to the Butterfly Rainforest**

Who doesn't love butterflies? Take your children to the [Butterfly Rainforest](https://sweetwaterinn.com/blog/butterfly-rainforest-gainesville/) while you're in town! Explore the 6,400-square-foot exhibit and marvel at more than 50 species of butterflies. Who knows, if you're lucky enough, one might just land on you!

#### **Unveil the Secret World of Bats at the Lubee Bat Conservancy**

We saved the best for last. One of our absolute favorite things to do in Gainesville with kids is to visit the [Lubee Bat Conservancy](https://sweetwaterinn.com/blog/lubee-bat-conservancy/). These animals are some of the cutest yet so unknown by the general public, and at the conservancy, you get to discover their secret world! This non-profit organization strives to protect the several bat species and is home to a conservation and research center, as well as over 200 bats. They offer [tours](https://www.lubee.org/battours) throughout the week, which usually last about two hours. Make sure you book in advance on their website as all tours need to be scheduled.**\
